Cybersecurity Challenge - Year 2

09/03/2021 - 10/03/2021

This is my second time participating in Belgium's Cybersecurity Challenge. Due to covid, this year's edition was planned to be completely online & remote. The first 60 teams would qualify for the next round, unlike the 43 from last year.

This year I formed a team with my fellow 2nd year students, under which were Shayne Declercq, Randy Bosmans, and Alexander Dekker. With last year's knowledge under my belt I was able to solve a good few challenges very quickly. The more difficult challenges weren't solved as quickly, largely due to my lack of expert knowledge in system penetration and hacking. Fortunately my fellow students were in the Cybersecurity Bachelor and could help out.

This year's challenges were notably harder than last year's, and we ended up on a shared 71st place. Right under the qualification line. This year wasn't as much of a learning experience as last year, it was however more fun and entertaining because I was able to solve some challenges myself.

Cybersecurity Challenge

10/03/2020 - 11/03/2021

The Cybersecurity Challenge is a yearly CTF-event hosted in Belgium by several educational institutions and the countries defence departement. During the challenge teams face each other and the clock whilst they find flags in various challenges and try to get as many points as possible. The teams have 32 hours during which they solve various challenges related to cybersecurity. This year only the top 43 teams make the cut to move on to the pre-finals.

My team consisted of me and several 2nd year Howest Cybersecurity Specialist students such as Rafael De Smet en Sandro Deblonde. Unfortunately our team placed 51st and was not able to qualify for the next round.

As this was my first CTF-event, I had no experience with things like steganography and pentesting. Throughout this event I couldn't really contribute to my team's progress due to my lack of experience. I was however able to learn a lot during this experience and I would like to try again next year.
